# TP53 Functional Analysis

## Overview
This project focuses on the analysis of the TP53 tumor suppressor protein using different bioinformatics tools. The aim is to understand conserved regions, structural features, and interaction networks of TP53.

## Objectives
- To retrieve TP53 protein sequence
- To identify homologous sequences using BLAST
- To perform multiple sequence alignment and identify conserved regions
- To construct a phylogenetic relationship between species
- To analyze protein–protein interactions
- To predict and visualize the 3D structure of TP53

## Methodology

### 1. Sequence Retrieval
TP53 protein sequence was obtained from UniProt in FASTA format.

### 2. BLAST Analysis
BLASTp was used to identify homologous protein sequences across different organisms.

### 3. Multiple Sequence Alignment
ClustalW was used to perform alignment and identify conserved regions.

### 4. Phylogenetic Analysis
MEGA software was used to construct a phylogenetic tree to study evolutionary relationships.

### 5. Protein–Protein Interaction
STRING database was used to analyze interaction partners of TP53.

### 6. Homology Modeling
SWISS-MODEL was used to predict the 3D structure of TP53 protein.

### 7. Structure Visualization
PyMOL was used to visualize the predicted protein structure.

## Conclusion
The analysis shows that TP53 contains highly conserved regions that are functionally important. These regions are likely involved in DNA binding and protein interactions. Mutations in these conserved regions may disrupt normal TP53 function, which can lead to disease conditions such as cancer.
